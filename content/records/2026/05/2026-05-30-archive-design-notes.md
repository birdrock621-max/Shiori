The bookshelf metaphor is valuable when it communicates accumulation. It becomes a problem when decoration makes records difficult to find or read.

## Principles

| Area | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Entrance | Use a memorable, atmospheric shelf and layered image |
| Catalogue | Prefer clear filters, stable labels, and strong contrast |
| Reader | Keep the line length narrow and typography quiet |
| Motion | Respect reduced-motion preferences |
| Scale | Generate static pages and avoid loading every interaction up front |

## Constraint

The site must remain useful with zero records, seven records, or several hundred records.
