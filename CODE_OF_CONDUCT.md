# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a respectful, harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of age, body, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, experience, nationality, appearance, race, religion, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic background.

## Expected behavior

- Respect other people’s time, context, and level of experience.
- Give specific, constructive feedback.
- Admit mistakes, learn, and correct them.
- Write documentation that remains accessible to newcomers and people working in another language.
- Treat privacy with unusual care because this project handles journals and activity records.
- Prefer decisions that improve the long-term health of the community.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, sexualized behavior, intimidation, discrimination, trolling, or personal attacks.
- Publishing or implying private or identifying information.
- Submitting malicious code, secrets, real private journals, or non-consensual personal content.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces where someone represents the Shiori community. Report problems to the maintainers without adding more private information to a public thread. Maintainers may warn, edit, remove, restrict, or permanently exclude participants according to severity and context.

Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is not accepted. Reporter and subject privacy will be protected where practical.

This document is adapted from the principles of Contributor Covenant 2.1.
